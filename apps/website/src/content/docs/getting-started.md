Getting started walks you through setting up Ganju from scratch, one short page
per step. Follow them in order the first time, or jump to whichever piece you
need. Here's what each page covers:

- **[Sign in](/docs/getting-started/sign-in)** — create your account and log in
  to Ganju.
- **[Create an organization & project](/docs/getting-started/organization-and-project)**
  — set up your team and your first workspace, and invite the people who'll help
  manage it.
- **[Prompts](/docs/getting-started/prompts)** — write the instructions that
  shape how your AI behaves.
- **[Resources](/docs/getting-started/resources)** — add the files and knowledge
  your AI can search.
- **[Tools](/docs/getting-started/tools)** — switch on the actions your AI can
  take, like email, calendar, and web search.
- **[Channels](/docs/getting-started/channels)** — offer your AI to clients and
  teams on the apps they already use: Slack, WhatsApp, Discord, Telegram, and
  more.
- **[Settings](/docs/getting-started/settings)** — manage members, choose the
  model your AI runs on, and handle billing.

Work through them and you'll have a live assistant ready to share through a
channel.

> Each item above is its own page in the sidebar under **Get started**. New here?
> Begin with **[Sign in](/docs/getting-started/sign-in)**.

## Go deeper

The pages above are the quick path. When you're ready to expand what your assistant
can do, each building block has a fuller guide:

- **[Organizations & projects](/docs/organizations-and-projects)** — how the
  organization/project structure works, and running Free and Pro side by side.
- **[Prompts](/docs/prompts)** — multi-turn templates, typed variables, and editing
  as JSON.
- **[Resources](/docs/resources)** — Google Drive, OneDrive, website crawls, and
  uploads, with semantic search and citations.
- **[Tools](/docs/tools)** — the full catalog: Gmail, Outlook, Slack, calendars,
  web search, GitHub, Notion, and your own HTTP endpoints.
- **[Channels](/docs/channels)** — per-platform setup for Telegram, WhatsApp,
  Slack, and Discord.
- **[MCP clients](/docs/mcp)** — connect Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, or VS Code to the
  same project.
- **[Settings](/docs/settings)** — plans and billing, members, and bringing your
  own model.
